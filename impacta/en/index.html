We seek to mobilize society around a complex and urgent challenge:

The link between people and their healthcare in Chile.

¿What is it?

Impacta is the public innovation challenges program of the Laboratorio de Gobierno. Through this program we aim to attract and encourage talent from beyond the government, inviting people to get involved in complex public challenges by bringing their new ideas, technologies, products or services.

The Laboratorio de Gobierno will be responsible for opening up new public challenges through its projects and research team, with precise objectives and scopes as the core of these new ideas.

These ideas will be accompanied and financed by the Laboratorio de Gobierno so that they become solutions able to develop minimum viable prototypes (MVPs) for real-life testing.

The Laboratorio de Gobierno invites you to participate in Impacta Salud, the public challenge in search of solutions that strengthen the model of comprehensive family and community health, the primary pillar of public health service in Chile.

How can we bring healthcare closer to people so as to relieve overcrowding in family health centers and promote disease prevention and self-care?

Quinta Bella

* 75% CESFAM nationwide are located in 114 urban communities, corresponding to 51% of the national population

* CESFAMs deliver healthcare based on a model of comprehensive care, focusing on family and community

* CESFAMs represent 25% of primary healthcare centers

* Early evidence shows a systemic problem of access

* The management is focused on daily contingency plans: the service follows a plan designed to meet day-to-day needs, rather than a medium to long term strategy

* How can we bring healthcare closer to people so as to relieve overcrowding in family health centers and promote disease prevention and self-care? We aim to transform the relationship between citizens and public health system.

Referential Data. Investigation by Innovation Projects team, Laboratorio de Gobierno

Applicants and prizes

Applicants

  • * Individuals
  • * Legal entities (small companies)
  • * Chilean citizens or foreign citizens
  • * Multidisciplinary teams

Prizes

  • * Maximum of 20 grants of $ 5,000,000 CLP each (aprox. US$ 7,000) to be awarded for:
    • - Travel and accomodation expenses for 45 days in Santiago, Chile
    • - Prototyping the solution
  • * Prizes: 3 winners with a prize of $50.000.000 CLP each (aprox. US$ 70,000)

Evaluation

  • * Team Profile
  • * Quality of the idea and level of innovation
  • * Solution-problem match
  • * Potential for implementation
  • * Potential Impact
  • * MVP Development Plan
  • * Potential for communication
  • * Conceptual and technical quality of the MVP
  • * Implementation Plan and piloting
  • * Economic viability
  • * Potential scalability

The above criteria will be used at different phases of the evaluation process, not all at once. They will be assessed with different weightings. To see the evaluation table, download the challenge rules
